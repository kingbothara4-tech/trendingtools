Why people are breaking FDs and Investing in Gold and Silver?

Published: December 15, 2025

A big change is happening in how Indians save money. Fixed deposits are losing their shine.

üè¶ The Big Change in Indian Savings

Fixed deposits (FDs) were king in India for years. Everyone put money in FDs. It was safe and easy. But in 2025, something different is happening.

People are quietly moving money from FDs to gold and silver. They are not telling everyone. But the shift is real. Why? Because FD interest is too low now.

üí° Simple Truth: After tax, FD returns don't beat inflation. Your money actually loses value in the bank. Gold and silver keep their value better.

üìâ Why FD Returns Are Too Low Now

Let's understand this simply. In 2025, most banks give 6-7% interest on FDs. Sounds okay? But wait.

First, you pay tax on this interest. So your real return is around 4-5%. Now check inflation. Inflation is around 5-6%. This means prices of things go up faster than your money grows.

So your savings actually lose value. People are realizing this. That's why they are looking for better options.

üí∏ Gold vs FD: Which Gives More?

Look at the last 5 years:

  • Gold gave about 10-12% per year
  • FD gave 6-7% before tax
  • After tax, FD gave only 4-5%

The difference is clear. Gold wins. But it's not just about returns. Gold is something you can touch. In bad times, people trust gold more than bank numbers.

ü•á 5 Simple Reasons for the Shift to Gold & Silver

  1. FD interest too low - After tax and inflation, you lose money
  2. Gold is trusted - For centuries, Indians trust gold in crises
  3. Easy to buy now - Apps like PhonePe, Google Pay sell gold with one click
  4. Global uncertainty - When world economy is shaky, gold does well
  5. Silver is cheaper - Young people can buy silver easily, it's more affordable

I see two types of buyers. Old people still buy gold jewelry. Young people buy digital gold or silver through apps. Both are moving away from FDs.

üîÑ How Indians Are Moving Money Quietly

They are not taking out all money from FDs. That would be risky. Instead, they do this:

  • When old FDs mature, they move some money to gold
  • They put new savings directly in gold/silver, not in new FDs
  • They buy small amounts every month through apps
  • They tell no one - it's their secret savings plan

Jewelers say more people are buying gold coins. Banks say gold bond sales are up. The signs are everywhere if you look.

ü§î What Should You Do?

Should you also move money from FDs to gold? Maybe. But be smart about it.

Here's my simple advice:

  1. Keep some money in FDs for emergencies
  2. Put 10-20% of savings in gold or silver
  3. Don't put all money in one place
  4. Start small - buy 1 gram gold or small silver bar

Gold prices go up and down. So don't panic if price falls. Think long term. Indians are learning this. That's why the shift is quiet, not rushed.

‚ùì Common Questions Answered Simply

Is gold safer than fixed deposits?
In long term, yes. Gold keeps value better. But keep both - some in FD for quick needs, some in gold for future.
How to start buying gold?
Three easy ways: 1. Buy gold coins from bank 2. Buy digital gold on PhonePe/Paytm 3. Buy from local jeweler you trust.

Nobody knows. But history shows gold prices rise over time. Don't worry about short term ups and downs.

Money matters are private. Also, they don't want banks to know they're moving money out. It's personal choice.
